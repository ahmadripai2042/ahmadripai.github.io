About Me

Salam Kenal

Ahmad Ripai was born and grew up in Muara Botung, a small village in Mandailing Natal, North Sumatra, Indonesia. He completed his undergraduate studies at the Department of Physics, Universitas Andalas, in 2019, with a thesis supervised by Dr. Zulfi Abdullah and Dr. Mahdhivan Syafwan. During his undergraduate years, he also pursued his postgraduate studies (master's degree) through a fast-track program. As a result, he successfully earned both his bachelor's and master's degrees in just 4 years and 10 months, graduating with his master's degree in 2020. He works in theoretical physics, focusing on soliton and other nonlinear phenomena. While working on his master's thesis, he was additionally supervised by Dr. Wahyu Hidayat from Bandung Institute of Technology.

Ahmad is currently pursuing a PhD in Theoretical Physics and Complex Systems at the Department of Physics, IPB University, under the supervision of Prof. Dr. Husin Alatas and Dr. Albertus Sulaiman. His PhD research focuses on nonlinear wave dynamics and atmospheric circulation in coastal and peatland areas of Sumatra. He also works as a research assistant under Dr. Albertus Sulaiman in the Theoretical Fluid Dynamics of Indonesian Maritime Continent (IMC) research group at the Research Center for Climate and Atmosphere, National Research and Innovation Agency (BRIN), Indonesia.
